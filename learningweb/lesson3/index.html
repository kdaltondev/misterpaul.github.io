Lots of new things here!
  1. in the previous lesson, we used <p> for each of our paragraphs. In this lesson, we use <div> instead. I don't really know the difference. You could google it, maybe search for "difference between p and div in HTML"
  2. this page has images in it. You pull in images with the <img> tag.
  3. There are different kinds of images. Our first image is a SVG (scaleable vector graphic) file and the second is a PNG (google it - I'm not sure what the N is). You've also heard of GIF and maybe BMP (bitmap) files. And there are many more.
  4. You also should see <ol> and <li> tags.
Activities
  1. Play around with the width and height in each image. Make them small, make them huge. Change the height/width ratio (do you know what that is?) Which image scales better?
  2. What happens when you change an <ol> to <ul>? What do you think <ol>, <ul> and <li> mean? Google them and find out.
  3. Challenge: create a style in the style sheet for your list items. What can you set? Can you find a list of things you can set? You might find it somewhere in the developer tools, or you can google.
The most important thing is to experiment with scaling the images.
